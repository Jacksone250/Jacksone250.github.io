Proximity

Company/Organization: YouTube

Website Link: YouTube

Description:

As I look at the YouTube mobile page, they have taken a lot of care to the proximity of their elements. Videos and their accompanying metadata are close to the video along with the additionaloptions for the video. Next the main navigation tabs are all closely situated near the bottom accessible to the user immediately. Finally, the company logo, search bar, and user account are located at the top making up the final grouping of items.

Rule of Thirds

Company/Organization: City of Rexburg

Website Link: Rexburg

Description:

As I looked at the Rexburg homepage, I noticed that there were three distinct sections to the immediate page. Starting from the top, there is the Title with the menu button and search bar. Next there is a nice picture of the local area with a large link for those new to the area. Finally, we see that there is another group of often used links that are immediately available for users to choose from.

Repetition

Company/Organization: Craigslist

Website Link: Craigslist

Description:

Many of us have probably visited Craigslist at one point or another, but if not the homepage for a workstation is mainly just a bunch of lists separated into various categories for users to browse. Thankfully, when they made their mobile page, they compressed these lists into expandable sections underneath the main categories. This allowed them to clean up the mobile webpage and turn it into a simple list of categories that can be easily opened and closed at the user's desire. This process is a simple and repetitive process for users to easily get used to when they navigate the website. It also allows the repetition to continue underneath the initial categories and not overwhelm the user immediately.
